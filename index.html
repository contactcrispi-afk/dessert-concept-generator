Local Marketing Guide

Sell Your Bakes in
Facebook Groups

No ads, no budget needed. Here's exactly how to post in local Facebook groups and get real orders from your community.

Why Facebook Groups Work
Local buy/sell and community groups are full of people already looking to support home bakers. You just need to show up the right way.
1

Find the Right Groups to Join

Search Facebook for your city or town name + these keywords: "Buy Sell Trade", "Community Board", "Marketplace", "Foodie", "Moms of [your town]", or "[Your Town] Locals." Join 5–10 active groups before you post anything.

💡 Spend a few days liking and commenting on posts before you sell. Groups warm up to familiar faces, not strangers dropping links.
2

Write a Post That Feels Personal, Not Salesy

The biggest mistake home bakers make is writing a post that sounds like a flyer. Local groups respond to real people, not businesses. Lead with your story, not your menu.

✏️ Copy-Paste Template
Hey [Town Name] neighbors! 👋 I'm a home baker here in [town] and I just finished a fresh batch of [your dessert] — and honestly they turned out too good not to share. I bake out of my home kitchen using [something personal — real butter, family recipes, no preservatives, etc.] and I take small custom orders for local pickup. This week I have: 🍪 [Dessert 1] — $[price] 🍪 [Dessert 2] — $[price] 🍪 [Dessert 3] — $[price] DM me to place an order or drop a 🙋 below and I'll reach out! Pickup available in [neighborhood/area]. [Your first name] 🧁
3

Photos Are Everything — Here's the Easy Way

You don't need a ring light or a professional camera. Natural window light + your phone + a clean surface is all you need. Shoot from directly above (flat lay) or at a slight angle. Always include your hand or a bite taken — it makes people feel like they're already eating it.

💡 Post your photos in the actual Facebook comment section after your main post goes live. Facebook's algorithm shows posts with photos in comments more than posts with photos attached.
4

The Best Times to Post

Local community groups are most active on Thursday evenings, Friday mornings, and Sunday afternoons. Post your "this week's menu" on Thursday so people can plan weekend pickup. Post again Sunday for any leftovers or next-week pre-orders.

💡 Always reply to every comment — even just a heart emoji. Engagement keeps your post visible in the group feed longer.
5

Handle Orders Like a Pro

When someone comments, move them to DMs immediately. Confirm the order, share your Venmo/CashApp/PayPal, and give them a pickup window.

✏️ DM Reply Template
Hi [Name]! So glad you're interested 🧁 I'd love to bake for you! Here's what I have available this week: [List items + prices] I do pickup from [general area] — what day works best for you? Payment via [Venmo/CashApp/PayPal] at pickup. Let me know and I'll get you on the list! 🍪
Do's & Don'ts of Group Posting
Follow these and you'll build a loyal local customer base fast.

✅ Do This

  • ✓ Post consistently — same day each week
  • ✓ Use your real name and face
  • ✓ Tag the specific pickup neighborhood
  • ✓ Share behind-the-scenes baking photos
  • ✓ Respond to every comment quickly
  • ✓ Ask happy customers to post a review photo
  • ✓ Mention limited quantities to create urgency

🚫 Avoid This

  • ✗ Posting prices without photos
  • ✗ Copy-pasting the same post every week
  • ✗ Posting in groups that ban selling
  • ✗ Using stock photos instead of your own
  • ✗ Ignoring comments or going quiet
  • ✗ Making it sound like a formal business ad
  • ✗ Posting and ghosting — stay in the thread
